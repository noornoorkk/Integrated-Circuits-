This project focuses on the design and analysis of a low-power master-slave D flip-flop using Gate Diffusion Input (GDI) logic as an alternative to traditional CMOS design. The goal was to reduce power consumption, transistor count, and chip area while maintaining reliable performance. The flip-flop was implemented at the transistor level using Electric EDA tools, and its behavior was verified through simulations in LTspice.

Both CMOS-based and GDI-based designs were developed and compared in terms of power, delay, and efficiency. The results showed that the GDI approach significantly reduces power usage and area, making it suitable for modern low-power applications, although with slight trade-offs in timing performance. Overall, the project demonstrates how alternative logic styles like GDI can improve energy efficiency in digital integrated circuits.
